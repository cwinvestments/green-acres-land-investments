9. DEFAULT

If Purchaser fails to make any payment when due under this contract, a late fee of $75.00 shall be assessed after a seven (7) day grace period, as specified in Section 16.

If Purchaser remains in default for thirty (30) days or more after the payment due date, Seller may, at Seller's sole discretion, send Purchaser a written Default and Cure Notice by certified mail. Upon sending such notice, a notice fee of $75.00 plus actual postal costs shall be added to the amount due.

Upon receipt of a Default and Cure Notice, Purchaser shall have fourteen (14) days to cure the default by paying all amounts due, including missed payments, late fees, notice fees, and postal costs. If Purchaser fails to cure the default within the 14-day cure period, Seller shall have the following remedies, at Seller's sole discretion:

(a) Cancel this contract, declare all payments made by Purchaser forfeited as liquidated damages, and regain immediate possession of the property; or

(b) Declare the entire unpaid principal balance and all accrued interest immediately due and payable, and take appropriate legal action for collection according to the laws of the State of {{STATE}}.

In the event of default and termination of this contract by Seller, Purchaser shall forfeit any and all payments made under the terms of this contract, including taxes and assessments, as liquidated damages. Seller shall be entitled to recover such other damages as may be due which are caused by the acts or negligence of Purchaser.

The parties expressly agree that in the event of default not cured by Purchaser and termination of this agreement, if Purchaser fails to vacate the premises, Seller shall have the right to obtain possession by appropriate court action, including but not limited to eviction proceedings.
